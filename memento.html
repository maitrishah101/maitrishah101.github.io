Memento

A part of the application for Berkeley Innovation was to work on a weeklong Human Centered Design project. The prompt was: On average, a typical middle-class American wastes 1 lb. of food every day. How might we improve food waste management?

I have outlined my project below, which I worked on during Spring 2019. I started knowing nothing about the design/HCD process but by the end of the project I felt like I learned a lot.

Title Slide

I first started out by outlining the problem and explaining why food waste is bad. I also tried to research why people waste food in the first place.

Step 1 of the HCD process is to empathize with the user and look into the reasons behind food wastage from a user's perspective. I also wanted to look into current products/services available so I wouldn't come up with a solution that already exists.

I came up with a survey to understand people's food waste habits and the motivations behind them. I received a little over 50 responses. This slide contains some of the most insightful questions I asked.

Step 2 of the HCD process is to find key insights from research. I analyzed all the data and this is what I found! This slide is my favorite: the proportion of fruit shown represents the statistics that are presented.

I consolidated my reserach findings into 5 personas that represented people's actions.

For each of my personas, I developed a journey map of their actions, so I could empathize with each user and understand their thought processes.

Step 3 of the HCD process is to ideate. I came up with a number of different solutions that I felt addressed the main problems people were facing.

Step 4 of the HCD process was to choose a solution and prototype it. I decided to call my solution "Memento" or "Remember" in Latin. This was an attempt to help people remember their food.

Here are some more prototypes of the application. This was my first time prototyping. I would try to make the screens more consistent and remove the background from the iPhone.

I wanted to wrap up by thanking Berkeley Innovation for this opportunity. In one short week I learnt a lot about the HCD process, and food waste was a really interesting problem to solve.
