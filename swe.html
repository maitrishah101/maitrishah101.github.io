Berkeley Program Spring 2019

SWE++ is a committee that I helped start under the Society of Women Engineers at Berkeley. Our goal is to provide quality computer science education to middle school girls in the area. In Fall 2018 (our first semester), we taught around 30 middle schoolers basic Python skills and provided them with a supportive, inclusive, and fun environment to learn in. Our students came from 10 middle schools, many of which didn't provide a proper computer science education. At the end of the semester, we hosted a day long event, "Code Day", for our students and their friends where they participated in a series of workshops.

In Spring 2019, we taught another amazing batch of students and expanded our program to San Jose State University and Santa Clara University. Both programs were really successful.

We are currently expanding to other SWE chapters across the country! If you want to learn more about my experience coming up with the idea, preparing for our first semester, and building SWE++ into what it is now, check out this Medium article I wrote!
